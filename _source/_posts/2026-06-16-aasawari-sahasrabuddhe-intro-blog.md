## From software developer to developer relations (DevRel): how it started

Taylor Swift once said: Sometimes walking out is one thing that will find you the right thing. That's probably what happened to me. During my engineering days, all I ever wanted was to be a backend engineer, write code all day, use dark mode for IDEs, and sip coffee. And it all happened. I started as a software developer focused on shipping features, debugging all night and building new features again. However, something felt incomplete and being part of developer relations changed that.

{% img blog/aasawari-sahasrabuddhe-intro-blog/community-collage.jpg alt:"Aasawari speaking at conferences and working with developer communities" width:"800" %}{: .center-image }

I was always an educator, into public speaking, building ideas and strategies. And with developer relations (DevRel), I get to convert all that into work. Fortunately, I had the opportunity to wear multiple hats: community engineer, content creator, strategist and workshop builder. And now I get to do code, community, and content, everything together.

What made me stay in the community was the feeling of "Oh, that's exactly what I was looking for. Thank you for guiding me." Once someone said this, and this became my calling. Hence, I enjoy being here, closer to the community.

## My technical journey: Java, Spring Boot, and beyond

Over the years, I've written technical content, led hands-on workshops, produced YouTube videos, and contributed as a guest author and MongoDB Champion. My technical home base is Java, and frameworks like Quarkus and Spring Boot have always been special to me. They've been with me since my undergraduate days and have never left. Along the way in my engineering journey, another technology that prompted a deep dive was containers; topics such as Kubernetes, Docker, continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD), and GitOps all piqued my interest. I am also recognised as the MongoDB Champion for the contributions made to the community over the years.

## Why I'm joining Okta: identity, security, and AI

And now? I'm joining Okta as an AI Builder Advocate.

When I first thought about Okta, I thought: authentication, two-factor authentication and login flows. All important. But the more I dug in, the more I realised Okta is at the centre of something much bigger. Identity is quietly becoming the foundation of how AI systems are trusted, accessed, and governed. That intersection of security, identity, and AI is where I want to be, and I couldn't be more excited to explore it with all of you. While I am learning about Okta and its products, I am excited to talk about what the community is building with AI and Okta and how we can help you in your journey.
